# Leverage Analysis Skill

When answering leverage risk questions over SEC filings:

## Required Evidence

**Categories**:
- debt
- equity
- interest_expense
- numeric_facts

**Sections**:
- Consolidated Balance Sheet
- Consolidated Statement of Income
- Management's Discussion and Analysis

## Key Metrics

- Total debt (all debt instruments)
- Shareholders' equity
- Long-term debt (non-current portion)
- Interest expense
- Net income / Earnings

## Calculated Ratios

- Debt-to-Equity Ratio = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
- Debt-to-Assets Ratio = Total Debt / Total Assets
- Interest Coverage Ratio = Net Income / Interest Expense

## Research Steps

1. Extract total debt from balance sheet (both years).
2. Extract shareholders' equity from balance sheet (both years).
3. Extract interest expense from income statement (both years).
4. Calculate leverage ratios for each year.
5. Analyze change: is company becoming more or less leveraged?
6. Verify calculations are mathematically correct.
7. Do not claim solvency risk without evidence-based reasoning.
8. If leverage is increasing, verify that coverage ratios remain adequate.
9. Note any significant equity issuances or debt issuances that explain ratio changes.
10. Unsupported leverage conclusions must be excluded.

## Scoring Criteria

- Ratio calculation: correct and verified
- Trend analysis: both years present, change explained
- Context: leverage change placed in peer/industry context if available
- Limitations: noted (e.g., "off-balance-sheet leases not included in debt")
