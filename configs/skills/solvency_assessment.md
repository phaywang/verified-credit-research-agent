# Solvency Assessment Skill

When answering solvency/short-term liquidity questions over SEC filings:

## Required Evidence

**Categories**:
- liquidity
- working_capital
- cash_flow
- numeric_facts

**Sections**:
- Consolidated Balance Sheet
- Consolidated Statement of Cash Flows
- Management's Discussion and Analysis

## Key Metrics

- Current assets
- Current liabilities
- Inventory (may be illiquid)
- Operating cash flow
- Free cash flow
- Working capital trends

## Calculated Ratios

- Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- Quick Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
- Cash Flow Ratio = Operating Cash Flow / Current Liabilities

## Research Steps

1. Extract current assets and current liabilities from balance sheet (both years).
2. Calculate current and quick ratios for each year.
3. Extract operating cash flow from cash flow statement (both years).
4. Analyze working capital trends: is it improving or deteriorating?
5. Assess operating cash flow adequacy: can company cover current liabilities?
6. Do not claim insolvency without evidence of actual cash shortfall or negative trends.
7. If ratios worsen, verify that this reflects operational issues, not restructuring.
8. Note seasonal patterns if applicable (e.g., retail companies).
9. Provide specific metrics with calculations shown.
10. Unsupported solvency conclusions must be excluded.

## Scoring Criteria

- Ratio calculation: correct and verified
- Trend analysis: both years present, direction clear
- Cash flow support: OCF trend aligns with ratio trend
- Context: ratios compared to prior years and peers if available
- Limitations: noted (e.g., "excludes contingent liabilities")
