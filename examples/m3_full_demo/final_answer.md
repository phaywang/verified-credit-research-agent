# Ford Motor Company: Debt & Liquidity Credit Research Brief
**Period: 2023 vs. 2025 | Company Excluding Ford Credit vs. Consolidated**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Ford's credit profile from 2023 to 2025 presents a mixed picture that resists a simple "improved" or "deteriorated" characterization. At the Company excluding Ford Credit level, total debt rose modestly while its maturity structure shifted materially toward the near term — a meaningful increase in refinancing and liquidity pressure. Simultaneously, Ford's headline liquidity measure (Company liquidity) expanded, and balance sheet cash held roughly steady. Ford Credit's liquidity sources contracted slightly but remained substantial. The net credit assessment is one of **increased near-term debt risk offset by a larger committed liquidity buffer at the Company level**, with Ford Credit showing modest but not alarming liquidity erosion.

---

## 2. Debt Risk Changes

**Company Excluding Ford Credit — Total Debt**

Total debt for Company excluding Ford Credit rose from $19.944 billion in 2023 to $21.919 billion in 2025, an increase of $1.975 billion (+9.9%) [verified: claim_company_debt_excluding_ford_credit_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_debt_009; F_2025_10K_debt_008).

**Maturity Structure Shift — The Key Credit Concern**

The more significant development is not the level of total debt but its maturity composition, which shifted sharply toward the near term:

- Debt payable within one year increased from $0.477 billion in 2023 to $5.550 billion in 2025, a rise of $5.073 billion (+1,063.5%) [verified: claim_company_debt_payable_within_one_year_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_debt_009; F_2025_10K_debt_008).

- Long-term debt payable after one year declined from $19.467 billion in 2023 to $16.369 billion in 2025, a reduction of $3.098 billion (−15.9%) [verified: claim_company_long_term_debt_payable_after_one_year_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_debt_009; F_2025_10K_debt_008).

- Short-term borrowings specifically rose from $0.362 billion in 2023 to $1.355 billion in 2025, an increase of $0.993 billion (+274.3%) [verified: claim_company_short_term_borrowings_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_debt_009; F_2025_10K_debt_008).


**Credit Interpretation:** The decline in long-term debt is structurally positive in isolation — it reduces future interest burden and leverage over the medium term — but the near-simultaneous surge in current maturities creates a concentrated refinancing requirement in the near term. This represents an elevated, time-specific debt risk rather than a broad deterioration in leverage.

## 3. Liquidity Risk Changes

Liquidity metrics at the Company excluding Ford Credit level moved in **different directions**, and both directions matter for credit analysis.

**Company Cash (Excluding Ford Credit)**

Company cash (cash equivalents, marketable securities, and restricted cash, excluding Ford Credit) was essentially flat: $28.8 billion in 2023 versus $28.7 billion in 2025, a decline of $0.1 billion (−0.35%) [verified: claim_company_cash_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_debt_002; F_2025_10K_liquidity_001). On a standalone basis, this near-stability is modestly reassuring given the increase in near-term debt obligations.

**Total Balance Sheet Cash (Including Ford Credit)**

Total balance sheet cash, cash equivalents, marketable securities, and restricted cash — including Ford Credit and entities held for sale — declined from $40.4 billion in 2023 to $38.9 billion in 2025, a decrease of $1.5 billion (−3.7%) [verified: claim_total_balance_sheet_cash_and_marketable_securities_restricted_cash_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_liquidity_001; F_2025_10K_liquidity_001). This consolidated metric reflects modest cash consumption across the enterprise.

**Company Liquidity (Excluding Ford Credit)**

Company liquidity — which adds total available committed credit lines to Company cash, less restricted cash — increased from $46.4 billion in 2023 to $49.8 billion in 2025, a gain of $3.4 billion (+7.3%) [verified: claim_company_liquidity_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_debt_002; F_2025_10K_liquidity_001). This expansion suggests Ford has grown its committed credit line availability, partially offsetting the near-term debt maturity pressure.

**Ford Credit Liquidity**

Ford Credit's liquidity metrics contracted modestly but remained large in absolute terms:

- Ford Credit net liquidity available for use declined from $25.7 billion in 2023 to $24.6 billion in 2025, a decrease of $1.1 billion (−4.3%) [verified: claim_ford_credit_net_liquidity_available_for_use_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_credit_facilities_002; F_2025_10K_credit_facilities_002).

- Ford Credit total liquidity sources declined from $56.2 billion in 2023 to $54.4 billion in 2025, a decrease of $1.8 billion (−3.2%) [verified: claim_ford_credit_liquidity_sources_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_credit_facilities_002; F_2025_10K_credit_facilities_002).

- Ford Credit committed capacity was nearly unchanged, declining marginally from $45.3 billion in 2023 to $45.1 billion in 2025, a decrease of $0.2 billion (−0.4%) [verified: claim_ford_credit_committed_capacity_2023_2025] (source: F_2023_10K_credit_facilities_003; F_2025_10K_credit_facilities_003).

**Credit Interpretation:** The liquidity picture is genuinely mixed. Company cash is flat while Company liquidity is higher — the difference is attributable to expanded committed credit lines, which are contingent facilities rather than on-hand cash. Against a backdrop of $5.550 billion in debt payable within one year [verified: claim_company_debt_payable_within_one_year_2023_2025], the $28.7 billion in Company cash [verified: claim_company_cash_2023_2025] provides substantial nominal coverage, but the quality of that coverage depends on the liquidity and accessibility of the marketable securities and restricted cash components. Ford Credit's liquidity, while slightly reduced, remains deep and its committed capacity is essentially stable.

---

## 4. Management Explanation

Ford's 10-K filings define Company cash and Company liquidity as distinct metrics, explicitly excluding Ford Credit from both, reflecting the structural separation between the automotive manufacturing operations and the captive finance subsidiary (F_2025_10K_liquidity_001; F_2023_10K_liquidity_001). Management identifies Company liquidity — not cash alone — as the primary measure of financial flexibility for the Company excluding Ford Credit, incorporating committed credit line availability as a core component.


For Ford Credit, management notes that net liquidity available for use fluctuates quarterly based on near-term debt maturities, receivable growth and decline, and timing of funding transactions (F_2023_10K_credit_facilities_002), contextualizing the modest year-over-year decline as within normal operating variation rather than a structural impairment.

---

## 5. Evidence and Limitations

**Evidence Strengths**

- All debt figures are sourced from XBRL-tagged disclosures in Ford's 10-K filings with the SEC, providing high confidence in comparability (F_2023_10K_debt_009; F_2025_10K_debt_008).
- Liquidity figures are drawn from the Liquidity and Capital Resources section of each 10-K, which represents management's primary disclosure of these metrics (F_2023_10K_liquidity_001; F_2025_10K_liquidity_001).
- The maturity schedule excerpts (F_2023_10K_debt_011; F_2025_10K_debt_010) corroborate the aggregate debt figures and provide instrument-level detail supporting the maturity shift narrative.

**Limitations**

- Company cash of $28.7 billion [verified: claim_company_cash_2023_2025] includes restricted cash and marketable securities; the liquid, unrestricted portion available for debt repayment is not separately verified here and may be materially lower.
