**Executive Summary**

From 2023 to 2025, Ford's Company-level (excluding Ford Credit) debt increased modestly in total, but its maturity profile shifted sharply toward near-term obligations, raising refinancing risk. Headline liquidity improved, driven by expanded credit facility capacity rather than higher cash balances, which partially offsets the maturity risk.

---

**Debt Risk Changes**

Total Company debt excluding Ford Credit rose from $19.944B in 2023 to $21.919B in 2025 [verified: claim_company_debt_excluding_ford_credit_2023_2025], an increase of $1.975B or 9.9%. The more consequential change was in the maturity structure. Debt payable within one year increased from $0.477B in 2023 to $5.550B in 2025 [verified: claim_company_debt_payable_within_one_year_2023_2025], a rise of $5.073B. Simultaneously, long-term debt payable after one year fell from $19.467B in 2023 to $16.369B in 2025 [verified: claim_company_long_term_debt_payable_after_one_year_2023_2025], a reduction of $3.098B or 15.9%. This rotation from long-dated to short-dated obligations materially elevates near-term refinancing and rollover risk.

**Liquidity Risk Changes**

Total balance sheet cash, marketable securities, and restricted cash declined from $40.400B in 2023 to $38.900B in 2025 [verified: claim_total_balance_sheet_cash_and_marketable_securities_restricted_cash_2023_2025], a decrease of $1.500B. Despite this, total Company liquidity rose from $46.400B in 2023 to $49.800B in 2025 [verified: claim_company_liquidity_2023_2025], an increase of $3.400B or 7.3%. The divergence between falling cash and rising total liquidity indicates Ford expanded its undrawn committed credit facility capacity over the period, which provides a buffer but is contingent on facility availability and covenant compliance.
