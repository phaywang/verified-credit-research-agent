# Ford Debt and Liquidity Risk Brief

## Executive Summary
Company debt excluding Ford Credit increased from $19.944B in 2023 to $21.919B in 2025. The more credit-relevant movement was maturity mix: debt payable within one year rose sharply while long-term debt after one year declined, indicating more near-term refinancing/rollover exposure. Liquidity evidence is mixed by scope: total cash/marketable securities/restricted cash declined, while Company liquidity excluding Ford Credit increased.

## Debt Risk Changes
- Company debt excluding Ford Credit increased from $19.944B in 2023 to $21.919B in 2025, a +$1.975B change (+9.90%). [F_2023_10K_debt_009](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/37996/000003799624000009/f-20231231.htm) [F_2025_10K_debt_008](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/37996/000003799626000015/f-20251231.htm)
- Ford's Company excluding Ford Credit maturity mix worsened: debt payable within one year rose from $0.477B to $5.550B (+$5.073B, +1063.52%), while long-term debt payable after one year fell from $19.467B to $16.369B (-$3.098B, -15.91%). This combination points to higher near-term refinancing or rollover pressure, even though it does not by itself prove a liquidity shortfall. [F_2023_10K_debt_009](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/37996/000003799624000009/f-20231231.htm) [F_2025_10K_debt_008](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/37996/000003799626000015/f-20251231.htm)
- Company excluding Ford Credit short-term borrowings increased from $0.362B to $1.355B, a +$0.993B change (+274.31%). [F_2023_10K_debt_009](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/37996/000003799624000009/f-20231231.htm) [F_2025_10K_debt_008](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/37996/000003799626000015/f-20251231.htm)
